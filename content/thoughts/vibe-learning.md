i've never been a big proponent of tools taking away the fun of doing things. vibe-coding is a mixed bag for me. to me fun is solving a problem, optimizing stuff, designing a big picture, not telling agents to do the whole things for me. maybe boilerplating the boring part, like frontend components, html, and maybe scaffolding if possible? that's probably all to it. i get it with people vibecoding their project end-to-end, to see their imagination materialized. AI is here, it only gonna get better, sure.

i use chatgpt, claude for the usual 'google it away' lookup of understanding. i do use opencode/openrouter/minimax currently to prototype simple stuff, but not something i'd personally bet myself more on using. a simple claude chat (currently sonnex 4.6) is good enough for me to help ask, skim and confirm lot of information. jetbrains also have lot of ai assisting tooling, including chat and what not agents.

what i see myself in the future is, the augmentation of my learning process. like a peer-reviewer that goes with you as you learn. maybe one day there'll be a better tool to aggregate information during your learning process, build all the necessary tests for you to go through during your learning. isn't it great. just call it vibe learning.

maybe one day i wanna build a better learning co-pilot. or maybe just a simpler google next to me. a simple flash card app maybe? lol. not another AI app.
